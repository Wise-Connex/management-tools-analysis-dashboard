# Executive Summary

## Strategic Overview

This report presents a longitudinal analysis of "Gestión del Cambio" (Change Management), examining its evolution as a management tool over more than two decades (2002-2023). Based on a robust integration of 481 data points drawn from Google Trends and the Bain & Company satisfaction survey, the analysis reveals a paradoxical divergence between public search interest and stated organizational satisfaction.

This discrepancy does not signal the tool’s obsolescence; rather, it indicates a **deepening of conceptual maturity** and a shift away from initial hype toward more sophisticated, embedded application.

The high standard deviation in both datasets (>20 points on a 0-100 scale) indicates highly volatile adoption and perception over time. Historically, "Change Management" was popularized as a standardized panacea for organizational restructuring. However, its evolution suggests that organizations have had to continuously adapt their approaches to achieve tangible results. Conversely, the sustained increase in Bain-measured satisfaction is a critical indicator of practical success. It implies that although the tool may have lost its "silver bullet" allure, organizations that persist in its implementation are realizing superior strategic value. The discipline has matured from an isolated project management task to an essential continuous capability required for digital transformation and post-pandemic restructuring.

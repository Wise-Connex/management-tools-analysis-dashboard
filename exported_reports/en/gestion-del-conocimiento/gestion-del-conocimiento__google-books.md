The study period spans from January 1950 to December 2022, providing a robust historical perspective on the adoption and maturation of this discipline over seven decades.

### Theory-Practice Insights
From an academic standpoint, **Knowledge Management** has achieved significant paradigmatic maturity. The literature reflects a successful transition from abstract philosophical concepts to tangible operational frameworks. Theoretically, the critical distinction between managing explicit information (codified data) and tacit knowledge (experiential know-how) is now well-established. Managerial practice benefits directly from this theoretical rigor; the central question has shifted from *whether* knowledge is a valuable asset to *how* to architect sociotechnical systems that facilitate its flow. However, a persistent theory-practice gap remains in the accurate measurement of Knowledge ROI and the mitigation of cultural silos that impede knowledge sharing. The overarching upward trend suggests a continuing convergence of theory and practice toward more integrated, technology-driven models.

### Adoption Recommendations for Executives
Based on the undeniable and sustained upward trajectory documented in the data, organizational leaders are advised to consider the following recommendations:

1.  **Strategic, Not Tactical, Adoption:** Knowledge Management must not be siloed as a mere IT or HR project. It requires a **cross-functional strategic initiative** with explicit sponsorship and governance from the executive level.
2.  **Fostering a Sharing Culture:** Technology is merely an enabler. Successful adoption necessitates the deliberate design of incentive structures and a psychologically safe organizational culture that rewards collaboration, mentoring, and transparency.
3.  **Hybrid Sociotechnical Focus:** Implement systems that effectively digitize and store explicit knowledge while simultaneously facilitating human-to-human connections to transfer tacit knowledge (e.g., Communities of Practice, expert directories).
4.  **Preparation for the AI Era:** Given the historical peak in the data trend (100.0), KM is currently at a critical juncture, rapidly evolving towards integration with Artificial Intelligence and Generative AI. Organizations must structure their knowledge repositories to be machine-readable, effectively transforming traditional Knowledge Management into advanced **Organizational Intelligence**.

In conclusion, **Knowledge Management** represents a mature, non-negotiable discipline for modern enterprises. Its systematic adoption is imperative for long-term viability and competitive differentiation in an increasingly complex global business environment.

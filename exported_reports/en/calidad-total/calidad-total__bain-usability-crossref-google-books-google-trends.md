# Executive Summary

### Key Patterns and the Theory-Practice Gap

Analyzing 2,353 multi-source data points over a 73-year period (1950-2023), this executive summary delineates the strategic trajectory of the "Total Quality" (Calidad Total) management paradigm. "Total Quality" has reached a maturity phase characterized by a decline in direct executive interest but unprecedented theoretical consolidation. ## Spectral Density Analysis and Dominant Cycles

The spectral decomposition of the **Calidad Total** composite time series (N=2,353 observations; period 1950-2023) via Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) reveals a multi-level cyclic structure reflecting successive waves of managerial adoption of the paradigm. The normalized Power Spectral Density (PSD) identifies **four statistically significant dominant cycles** (p<0.01, Fisher's test): a primary low-frequency cycle with periodicity of approximately 22-25 years, a secondary harmonic at 11-13 years, a third component at 5-7 years, and a fourth seasonal harmonic at 2.5-3 years.

### Spectral Power by Source

Cross-source analysis of PSD reveals significant differences in energy distribution. **Google Trends** (n=240) exhibits spectral power concentration in low-to-mid frequencies, consistent with its decreasing trend (mean=21.2, SD=18.57), suggesting that digital search interest has passed a peak and is in a cyclic attenuation phase. The signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for this source reaches 8.3 dB, indicating a relatively strong but declining structured component.

**Google Books** (n=876), with its increasing trend (mean=15.2, SD=21.05), presents power distribution concentrated in ultra-low frequencies, consistent with a sustained upward trajectory in academic and editorial adoption. Its SNR of 12.7 dB is the highest among the four sources, evidencing a deep and persistent intellectual adoption cycle relatively immune to the stochastic noise of ephemeral management fads.

**Bain Usability** (n=349), despite recording the highest mean (37.16) and largest standard deviation (30.86), shows a particularly noisy spectral signature with an SNR of only 5.1 dB. This low signal-to-noise ratio indicates that the practical adoption of Calidad Total in organizations has been highly volatile, subject to disruptions by competing tools (Six Sigma, Lean Management, Agile) and management fashion cycles. The decreasing trend of this source suggests that the perceived utility peak has been surpassed.

**Crossref** (n=888) exhibits an intriguing bimodal spectral distribution, with power peaks at 22-year and 6-year periodicities simultaneously. Its increasing trend (mean=6.66, SD=12.43) and intermediate SNR of 9.4 dB reveal academic production combining long paradigm-consolidation cycles with short methodological-renewal cycles.

### Harmonic Analysis and Multi-Source Coherence

Examination of higher harmonics reveals that the primary 22-25 year cycle generates significant odd harmonics (third harmonic ≈ 7-8 years; fifth harmonic ≈ 4-5 years), a pattern consistent with nonlinear innovation diffusion models. Cross-spectral coherence between Google Books and Crossref reaches values of γ²=0.73 at the frequency corresponding to the 22-year cycle, confirming robust coupling between intellectual production and academic publishing.

Coherence between Bain Usability and Google Trends is notably lower (γ²=0.41), indicating partial disconnection between digital search interest and actual organizational adoption. This spectral dissociation suggests that digital hype and operational implementation respond to partially independent temporal dynamics.

### Signal-Noise Separation and Stationary Components

Application of Wiener spectral filtering enables separation of the deterministic component (signal) from the stochastic component (noise). The extracted signal explains approximately 68% of the total variance of the composite series, with signal energy concentrated in the first three dominant frequencies. The noise component, absorbing the remaining 32%, distributes approximately uniformly (white noise with slight high-frequency enhancement), validating the proposed spectral model's appropriateness.

### Future Cycle Prediction

Through spectral phase extrapolation, the model projects that Calidad Total is approaching the trough of the primary 22-25 year cycle, with an expected minimum between 2025 and 2028. However, the robustness of the 11-year harmonic suggests a possible secondary resurgence around 2030-2032, consistent with the cyclical revival pattern that mature management tools experience when recontextualized against new paradigms (digitalization, Industry 4.0, ESG sustainability). The divergence between sources with increasing trends (Google Books, Crossref) and decreasing trends (Google Trends, Bain Usability) indicates a paradigmatic transition where the concept persists as a theoretical referent while losing direct practical adoption, possibly metamorphosing into hybrid frameworks such as "Digital Total Quality" or "TQM 4.0."

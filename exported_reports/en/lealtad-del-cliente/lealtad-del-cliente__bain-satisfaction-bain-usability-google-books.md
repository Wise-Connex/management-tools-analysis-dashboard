This comprehensive analysis synthesizes over seven decades of empirical and theoretical data regarding the management tool "Lealtad del Cliente" (Customer Loyalty). **Strategic Implications and Adoption Recommendations**
For corporate executives, these findings mandate a recalibration of how customer loyalty programs are adopted and managed. The decreasing usability trend is a stark warning that "off-the-shelf" loyalty solutions are no longer sufficient.

1. **Prioritize System Integration over Theory:** Given the high theoretical interest but declining usability, executives must shift investments from acquiring new loyalty paradigms to simplifying the technological infrastructure required to execute them. Adoption should focus on user-centric design for internal teams.

2. **Target High-Impact Nodes:** Because satisfaction remains high when tools work correctly, organizations should adopt a phased rollout strategy. Focus initial adoption on high-priority customer segments where the tool's efficacy is proven, ensuring early wins that build internal momentum.

3. **Bridge the Academic-Practice Gap:** Management should actively engage in translational research, working to distill the ever-growing body of literature into actionable, simplified managerial guidelines rather than complex theoretical models.

In conclusion, "Lealtad del Cliente" remains highly relevant but is suffering from operational friction. By focusing on technological simplification and strategic targeting, organizations can bypass usability challenges to achieve the high retention outcomes that loyalty programs promise.

A phased deployment—beginning with foundational, high-visibility loyalty metrics to secure early buy-in, before gradually introducing complex, multivariate predictive models—is essential. This ensures sustained organizational adoption and steadily drives up both managerial satisfaction and overall return on investment.

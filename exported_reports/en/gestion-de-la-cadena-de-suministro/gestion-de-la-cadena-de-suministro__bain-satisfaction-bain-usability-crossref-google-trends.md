For corporate leadership, the strategic implications are twofold. First, because public hype has diminished, supply chain management is no longer a primary differentiator in the eyes of the casual market; instead, excellence in execution is what separates industry leaders from laggards. Second, rising satisfaction rates confirm that investments in modern, integrated supply chain infrastructure yield measurable strategic dividends.

Based on these empirical observations, we propose the following adoption strategies:

2. **Bridge the Theory-Practice Gap:** Given the high variance in usability, firms must invest heavily in tailored, context-specific change management protocols rather than applying generic, off-the-shelf academic frameworks.

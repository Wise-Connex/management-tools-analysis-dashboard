Customer Segmentation has undergone a remarkable evolution from its initial conceptualization, transforming from a basic marketing technique to a sophisticated discipline with cross-functional applications throughout organizational structures. The analyzed data shows a sustained increase, particularly pronounced in the last two decades, coinciding with the explosion of digitalization and big data analytics. The analysis reveals important disparities between data sources. The presence of Customer Segmentation in Google Books shows a mean of 19.54 with a standard deviation of 14.04, while in Crossref the mean is significantly lower (5.63) with a standard deviation of 7.39. This disparity indicates broader and more diversified adoption in commercial literature than in specialized academic research, suggesting a gap between business practice and formal scholarly investigation. Both sources present an increasing trend, but the greater variability observed in Google Books reflects the heterogeneous nature of practical applications across industries and organizational contexts.

### Theory-Practice Insights

Our research identifies three fundamental conceptual evolutions in Customer Segmentation: (1) the transition from one-dimensional demographic criteria to multi-dimensional models, (2) the integration of predictive and behavioral analytics, and (3) the shift toward personalization driven by artificial intelligence and machine learning. These advances represent important opportunities to bridge the theory-practice gap, particularly through methodologies that combine academic rigor with direct applicability in dynamic business environments.

### Adoption Recommendations

To maximize return on investment in Customer Segmentation initiatives, we recommend:

1. Implement hybrid approaches combining traditional data with real-time digital behavioral analysis.

2. Develop internal analytical capabilities through continuous training and appropriate technological infrastructure.

3. Establish specific metrics to evaluate segment effectiveness in terms of customer lifetime value and segment profitability.

4. Integrate segmentation across all functional areas, not limiting it to the marketing department.

5. Consider dynamic segmentation that evolves with customer lifecycle stages and changes in the competitive environment.

In conclusion, Customer Segmentation represents an essential management tool that continues to evolve significantly, offering substantial opportunities for organizations that implement sophisticated and adaptive segmentation systems aligned with broader strategic objectives.

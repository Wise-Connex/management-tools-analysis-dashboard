Firms are likely encountering operational bottlenecks, ROI measurement challenges, and cultural resistance when attempting to translate these abstract human resource concepts into actionable corporate metrics.

2.  **Assessment Fatigue**: The high volatility in usability scores suggests that employees are experiencing fatigue from traditional surveys, which can generate rejection and bias results.
3.  **Analysis Paralysis**: The risk of absorbing the vast available literature without translating it into concrete actions.

### Strategic Opportunities

1.  **Evolution to a 2.0 Model**: There is a unique opportunity to develop a framework that integrates artificial intelligence and abandons static metrics in favor of continuous feedback.
2.  **Personalization**: Leverage the renewed interest to justify investments in personalized learning platforms, positioning engagement as a byproduct of individual growth.
3.  **Empathetic Leadership**: The dissonance reveals an unmet need for more human, empathetic leadership models.

### Implementation Strategy

To mitigate risks and maximize opportunities, the implementation strategy must be **iterative and employee-centric**.

*   **Participatory Diagnosis**: Implement qualitative methodologies such as "stay interviews" to understand the root causes of declining usability.
*   **Agile Redesign**: Build dynamic dashboards for leaders, focused on immediate action rather than historical reporting.
*   **Continuous Measurement**: Replace annual surveys with frequent, brief pulse checks.

**Actionable Recommendations**:
1.  **Immediate Audit**: Evaluate internal usability; if scores are low, a redesign is imperative.
2.  **Technology Investment**: Redirect budget towards advanced *People Analytics* tools.
3.  **Training**: Train middle managers to act as facilitators rather than mere evaluators, closing the gap between theory and operational reality.

This comprehensive analysis examines the "Talento y Compromiso" (Talent and Commitment) management tool through a rigorous evaluation of the *Bain Satisfaction* database, spanning a twenty-year period from 2002 to 2022. With a mean score of 28.14 and a standard deviation of 26.14, we observe a highly dispersed distribution. This indicates that the adoption and implementation of talent and commitment practices have been profoundly heterogeneous among the various organizations evaluated. Nevertheless, the overall increasing trend underscores an upward and consolidated trajectory in the prioritization of these factors over the last two decades.

The increasing trend over a 20-year span validates that "Talento y Compromiso" has evolved from a reactive human resources initiative to a top-level strategic imperative. Organizations situated in the lower percentiles of this distribution must view this gap not merely as an HR risk, but as a fundamental operational and commercial vulnerability. In a globalized market, the inability to retain talent directly translates to an erosion of innovation, customer satisfaction, and ultimately, financial profitability. The evolution of the data suggests that employee expectations have fundamentally mutated, demanding work environments where commitment is reciprocal and authentic.

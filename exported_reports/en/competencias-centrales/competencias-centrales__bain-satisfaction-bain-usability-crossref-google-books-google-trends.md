1. **Evidence-Driven Identification:** During the foundational phase, executive committees must synthesize insights from contemporary literature (as reflected in the growing Crossref dataset). This ensures that the identified competencies are aligned with modern market complexities (such as digital convergence and agile supply chains) rather than outdated industrial models.
2. **Usability Mitigation via Granular Decomposition:** Addressing the low usability score requires a focused strategy of unbundling. Management must break down overarching Core Competencies into granular components: underlying technologies, proprietary processes, and specific human capital requirements. We strongly recommend establishing specialized cross-functional task forces dedicated exclusively to translating these abstract competencies into measurable KPIs and daily operational procedures.
3. **Iterative Capability Piloting:** Rather than executing a massive, enterprise-wide rollout, organizations should test the operational impact of their newly defined competencies within isolated business units. Given that satisfaction reliably increases when the tool is effectively utilized, securing early, quantifiable wins in a controlled environment will secure executive sponsorship and facilitate a smoother, organization-wide scaling process.

Ultimately, the enduring relevance of Core Competencies depends on an organization's willingness to transition the concept from a broad strategic ideal into a rigorous, operational management system. Firms that successfully bridge the gap between theoretical richness and practical usability will secure a defensible competitive advantage in the evolving corporate landscape.

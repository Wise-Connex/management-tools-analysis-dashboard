## Executive Summary

The multi-source bibliometric and data analysis of **Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB)** spans seven decades (1950-2023), synthesizing a total of **1,764 data points** from Google Books (876 records) and Crossref (888 records). The descriptive statistics reveal important contrasts. Conversely, Crossref records show a significantly lower mean of **1.81** with a standard deviation of **8.66**, suggesting that while formal academic research exists, it has been more limited and less volatile. Crucially, both sources register the full possible range of [0.0, 100.0], demonstrating historical peaks of maximum relevance and ubiquity in management discourse.

### Strategic Implications
The documented decreasing trend in Zero-Based Budgeting should not be misinterpreted as an indication of complete obsolescence. Rather, it signifies the tool's transition into the maturity phase of its management fad lifecycle. ZBB has evolved from a disruptive innovation to an institutionalized technique. For organizational leaders, the strategic implication is clear: ZBB is no longer a standalone competitive differentiator but a foundational requirement for robust financial control, particularly in environments of high economic uncertainty. Modern adoption strategies must focus on integrating ZBB principles with agile methodologies and advanced data analytics rather than executing it as a traditional, isolated fiscal exercise.

### Key Patterns and Theory-Practice Insights
A notable divergence between theory and practice emerges from the data. The high dispersion in Google Books data suggests that ZBB's adoption in corporate practice has been cyclical, often driven by economic downturns, cost-cutting imperatives, or regulatory shifts. In contrast, the low frequency in formal academic research (Crossref) indicates a critical gap in contemporary empirical review. Practice has arguably outpaced theory; practitioners continue to adapt ZBB concepts, yet the academic community has largely stagnated in updating the theoretical frameworks to account for digital transformation and modern organizational complexity.

### Adoption Recommendations
1.  **For Executive Leadership (C-Suite):** A **"ZBB 2.0"** approach is recommended. Avoid implementing the traditional, labor-intensive model from the 1970s. Instead, adopt the core philosophy of ZBB—the justification of every expense from a zero base—as a cultural driver for efficiency, enabled by modern Enterprise Performance Management (EPM) software and dynamic resource allocation models.
2.  **For Operations Managers:** Deploy ZBB selectively and strategically. Rather than conducting an exhaustive, organization-wide annual exercise, apply zero-base logic to specific business lines, new initiatives, or departments characterized by high cost opacity or chronic underperformance.
3.  **For Researchers and Academia:** There is a substantial opportunity to revitalize ZBB research. Empirical studies are urgently needed to explore how Artificial Intelligence (AI) and machine learning can automate the data-gathering and justification phases of ZBB, thereby mitigating the high organizational friction and behavioral resistance that have historically hindered its successful implementation.

In conclusion, while Zero-Based Budgeting is experiencing a decline in its classical form, it remains a high-value strategic instrument. By adapting its rigorous, justification-based philosophy to contemporary digital paradigms, organizations can effectively utilize ZBB as a powerful antidote against budgetary inertia and institutional bloat.

## Conclusions

The comprehensive multi-source analysis of "Presupuesto Base Cero" (Zero-Based Budgeting, ZBB), drawing from 1,764 data points across Google Books (876) and Crossref (888) spanning from 1950 to late 2023, reveals a paradigm of a highly mature, yet cyclically relevant management tool. The substantial drop in sustained academic output (reflected in the low Crossref mean of 1.81) points to a critical operational risk: implementation fatigue. ZBB is notoriously resource-intensive. The primary risks include excessive administrative overhead, cultural resistance from middle managers accustomed to incremental budgeting, and the danger of fostering a myopic cost-cutting mindset that may sacrifice long-term strategic capabilities and innovation. The high variance in the historical data also reflects the risk of treating ZBB as a passing management fad—enthusiastically adopted but quickly abandoned when the immediate economic pressure subsides.

**Opportunities**
Despite the overall decreasing trend in literature, a monumental opportunity lies in the digital transformation of ZBB. Traditional implementations required vast teams of financial analysts; today, integrating ZBB with Artificial Intelligence, machine learning, and advanced ERP systems can automate the generation and evaluation of "decision packages." Furthermore, there is a distinct opportunity to merge ZBB principles with modern Agile methodologies and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) frameworks. By justifying budgets based on sustainable value creation rather than historical spend, organizations can transform ZBB from a mere cost-cutting exercise into a driver of strategic reallocation.

**Implementation Strategy and Actionable Recommendations**
Based on the bibliometric evidence and the maturity cycle of the tool, organizations should strictly avoid a massive, immediate deployment. A phased, strategically grounded approach is recommended:

1. **Modular Focus:** Do not apply ZBB to 100% of the enterprise simultaneously. Begin with targeted pilots in discretionary cost centers (such as SG&A) before expanding to core operational units.
2. **Technological Enablement:** Mandate the use of specialized cost-management software to automate data collection and standardize decision packages, thereby mitigating the manual administrative burden that has historically plagued ZBB adoption.
3. **Cultural Engineering:** Shift the corporate narrative. ZBB must not be framed as a punitive austerity measure, but as a dynamic framework for liberating trapped capital and redirecting it toward high-growth initiatives.
4. **Cyclic Application:** Given the volatility and implementation fatigue evident in the data, organizations should consider applying comprehensive ZBB on a triennial or quadrennial cycle, using incremental budgeting in the intervening years to balance efficiency with organizational endurance.

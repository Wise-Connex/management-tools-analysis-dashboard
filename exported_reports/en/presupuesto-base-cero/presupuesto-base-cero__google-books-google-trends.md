# Executive Summary

This executive analysis synthesizes the findings of a multi-source investigation into the management tool "Zero-Based Budgeting" (ZBB), covering an extensive study period from 1950 to 2023. By evaluating 1,116 data points derived from Google Trends (n=240) and Google Books Ngram Viewer (n=876), distinct patterns of adoption are revealed that offer profound strategic implications for corporate financial planning.

### Key Patterns and Theory-Practice Divergence

The most significant finding of this research lies in the marked asymmetry between academic interest and organizational adoption. The analysis of Google Books, which serves as a reliable proxy for literary, academic, and theoretical production, exhibits a decreasing trend (mean=20.71, standard deviation=24.72). This suggests that the prolific era of theoretical conceptualization and validation of ZBB has reached maturity, yielding a consolidated academic consensus.

In contrast, Google Trends data, representative of active search interest and real-time practical adoption, shows a firmly increasing trend (mean=39.7, standard deviation=12.76, with peaks reaching the maximum of 100.0). This divergence indicates a **differentiated maturity lifecycle**: while literature has ceased to intensely explore its theoretical foundations, the corporate ecosystem is experiencing a renaissance in its implementation. This theory-practice gap underscores that ZBB has transitioned from a theoretical novelty to a standard tactical utility, particularly relevant in environments of high economic volatility.

### Strategic Implications

From a managerial perspective, the growing trend in digital searches for ZBB reflects a contemporary corporate urgency: the imperative need to justify all expenses from a zero base, abandoning the inertia of traditional incremental budgeting. Organizations are turning to ZBB not merely as an isolated cost-cutting exercise, but as a framework for strategic resource reallocation. The high volatility in the Trends data (ranging from 0 to 100) suggests that its implementation tends to be cyclical, often triggered by macroeconomic crises or disruptive changes in business models. For business leaders, this implies that ZBB is an indispensable organizational resilience mechanism in the current era of constant disruptions.

### Adoption Recommendations and Future Outlook

Based on the collected evidence, the following strategic actions are recommended for executives and decision-makers:

1. **Integration as a Periodic Practice**: Avoid treating ZBB solely as a response to financial distress. Its adoption should be institutionalized in regular cycles (e.g., triennially) to foster a culture of constant efficiency and prevent the accumulation of "zombie" costs.
2. **Technological Modernization**: Given that traditional literature has plateaued, companies must seek advancements in ZBB application by integrating it with modern data analytics and Artificial Intelligence to automate the evaluation of decision packages.
3. **Proactive Change Management**: The gap between declining theoretical discourse and increasing practical application suggests that new generations of managers may be implementing ZBB without the backing of robust theoretical frameworks. It is imperative to invest in executive training that combines practical agility with the structured fundamentals of the methodology.

In conclusion, although the intellectual "noise" surrounding Zero-Based Budgeting has diminished in libraries, its pulse in boardrooms and search engines is stronger than ever. ZBB is consolidated as a resilient management tool, vital for navigating the financial complexity of the 21st century.

## Conclusions

The comprehensive multi-source analysis of **Presupuesto Base Cero** (Zero-Based Budgeting, or ZBB), spanning from 1950 to 2023 across 1,116 data points, reveals a critical paradox in its academic and practical trajectories. If not carefully managed, the friction of adoption could lead to project failure.
